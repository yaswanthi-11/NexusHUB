📞 Call Emergency (112)

When to Seek Emergency Care

If you or someone else is experiencing the following symptoms, call your local emergency number (e.g., 102, 108, or 911) immediately:

  • Chest Pain: Sudden pressure, squeezing, fullness, or pain in the center of the chest that lasts more than a few minutes, or that goes away and comes back.
  • Difficulty Breathing: Severe shortness of breath, gasping for air, or inability to speak in full sentences.
  • Stroke Signs (FAST): Facial drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call emergency services.
  • Severe Bleeding: Uncontrolled bleeding from a wound, especially if it's spurting or soaking through bandages.
  • Loss of Consciousness: Fainting, sudden confusion, unresponsiveness, or difficulty waking up.
  • Severe Allergic Reaction (Anaphylaxis): Swelling of the face or throat, difficulty breathing, hives, or a sudden drop in blood pressure.
  • Sudden Severe Pain: Especially in the head (thunderclap headache), abdomen, or back.
  • Poisoning or Overdose: If someone has ingested a toxic substance or taken too much medication.
  • Major Injuries: Such as from a car accident, fall from a height, or deep wounds.
  • Seizures: Especially if it's the first seizure, lasts longer than 5 minutes, or the person doesn't regain consciousness.

General Advice During an Emergency

While waiting for professional help, remember these general guidelines:

  • Stay Calm: Your calm demeanor can help the person in distress.
  • Do Not Move the Person: Unless they are in immediate danger (e.g., fire, falling debris).
  • Provide Basic First Aid: If you are trained and it's safe to do so.
  • Gather Information: Be ready to tell emergency services about the person's condition, medical history, and any medications.
  • Do Not Give Food or Drink: Unless instructed by emergency personnel.

Helpful Contacts & Resources

Keep these numbers saved in your phone and know your local resources:

  • National Emergency Number: 112 (Europe), 911 (North America), 108 (India) - *Call immediately for life-threatening situations.*
  • Ambulance Services: 102 / 108 (India), 999 (UK), 000 (Australia) - *Know your local number.*
  • Poison Control Center: Search online for your country's specific poison control hotline.
  • Nearest Hospital: Have the address and phone number of your closest emergency room readily available.
  • First Aid Training: Consider taking a certified first aid and CPR course.
